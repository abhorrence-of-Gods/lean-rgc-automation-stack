## 5. Clock classes and three-times margins

All child timing authority is integer `time.monotonic_ns()`.  No float seconds,
rounded milliseconds, wall clock, or cross-process timestamp subtraction is
used for a boundary decision.  The PowerShell parent uses integer
`System.Diagnostics.Stopwatch.GetTimestamp()` ticks with the exact stopwatch
frequency.  Child nanoseconds, parent ticks, and that exact frequency are
separate artifact fields.

| interval | class | clock authority | hard wall | success margin |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|
| Python process start through validated READY | `python_ready` | parent Stopwatch ticks | 15 s | at most 5 s |
| immediately before Lean `Popen` through strict `load_project` validation | `startup_load` | child monotonic ns | 120 s | at most 40 s |
| initial `status` | `control` | child monotonic ns | 30 s | at most 10 s |
| `init_state` including registered prefix tactics | `control_initialization` | child monotonic ns | 30 s | at most 10 s |
| `apply_tactic` | `action` | child monotonic ns | 15 s | at most 5 s |
| CLOSED-child `discard_state` | `control` | child monotonic ns | 30 s | at most 10 s |
| source `discard_state` | `control` | child monotonic ns | 30 s | at most 10 s |
| final `status` | `control` | child monotonic ns | 30 s | at most 10 s |
| shutdown write through ack validation and natural Lean exit 0 | `shutdown` | child monotonic ns | 10 s | at most 3 s |
| one fresh fixture through transient-result validation | `fixture_execution` | parent Stopwatch ticks | 300 s | at most 100 s |
| fake/fault unit runner | `unit_whole` | parent Stopwatch ticks | 30 s | at most 10 s |
| global preflight through archival transient-result validation | `batch_execution` | parent Stopwatch ticks | 1,200 s | at most 400 s |
| final artifact serialization through durable replacement and reread | `artifact_publication` | parent Stopwatch ticks, closeout only | 30 s | at most 10 s |

Every exact comparison is equivalent to `3 * elapsed <= hard_wall` except the
shutdown success margin, whose explicit 3-second bound is stricter.  Equality
at the success margin passes; one nanosecond above it blocks.  Hard-wall
exceedance is `SYNTHETIC_RESOURCE_BLOCKED`.  Hard-wall success with any margin
exceedance is the distinct disposition:

```text
SYNTHETIC_QUALIFICATION_MARGIN_BLOCKED
failure_code = QUALIFICATION_MARGIN_BLOCKED
```

The sum of child request success margins is 98 seconds, and the separate READY
margin adds up to 5 seconds.  The 100-second fixture gate is therefore an
intentionally stricter cross-clock gate and can block even when every
individual interval passes; it does not promise a positive overhead allowance.
The 400-second batch-execution gate is likewise intentionally stricter: passing
all six 100-second fixture gates does not imply batch success.  Publication is
a separate clock whose elapsed ticks are written only to the bundled closeout,
after the artifact bytes are already immutable; it is never self-attributed by
the artifact being timed.

The 120-second startup wall is adopted from the existing source-bound strict
client's control envelope.  Q0 observed only the censored lower bound of 15.052
seconds with zero stdout and stderr bytes before termination; its later
diagnostic overrun is disclosed but is not timing evidence.  The 120-second
wall is therefore a new v2 engineering envelope, not an estimate inferred from
a complete startup observation and not a claim that startup was previously
measured or proved below 120 seconds.

## 6. Exact timing endpoints

- `python_ready` begins immediately before the parent's exact Python `Start()`
  call and ends after strict READY semantic validation.
- `startup_load` begins immediately before the child's exact Lean `Popen` call,
  so `Popen` latency is included.  It ends only after the complete
  `load_project` response is read and its registered semantic witnesses pass.
- Each post-load frame begins immediately before the canonical request's stdin
  write and ends only after the full bounded response is read and all
  registered semantic validation finishes.
- `shutdown` begins immediately before the shutdown request write and ends only
  after ack validation and natural Lean process exit code 0.
- `fixture_execution` begins before that fixture's identity recheck and Python
  process start.  It ends after clean Python/Lean exit and strict transient-
  result revalidation, before final artifact serialization.
- `batch_execution` begins before global accepted-tree/identity preflight.  It
  includes final-path checks and mutex waiting, and ends after archival
  transient-result validation, before final artifact serialization.
- `artifact_publication` begins immediately before canonical artifact
  serialization and ends after durable replacement plus final reread
  validation.  Its parent ticks and final pass/block disposition are written
  to the bundled closeout, never into the artifact whose publication it times.

Every hard wall is an absolute deadline, not a fresh response timeout.
For child intervals, `deadline_ns = start_ns + hard_wall_ns`; Lean `Popen`,
request write, response wait, parsing, and semantic validation all consume the
same remaining duration, and the deadline is checked again after validation.
For parent intervals the identical rule uses Stopwatch ticks and frequency.
Fixture and batch remaining deadlines cap every nested wait.  Overflow or a
nonpositive remaining duration is an immediate resource block.

Negative, decreasing, overflowing, duplicated, reordered, or misclassified
timing evidence is invalid.  A completed process must contain READY and all
eight request intervals.  A failed process must contain READY when reached,
every completed interval in its exact ordered prefix, and the one terminal
failure interval when that interval began; no later frame may exist.  A gap in
that closed prefix is invalid.  The final parent-owned artifact envelope stores
those integer child values, child sequence, fixture execution, batch execution,
and the exact hard/margin policy map.  Parent and child classification maps
must be byte-identical.  Publication ticks and the parent Stopwatch frequency
are not conflated: fixture/batch ticks and the exact frequency are bound in the
artifact, while publication elapsed ticks occur only in the closeout and use
the same already-bound frequency.
